           Group technology is a manufacturing philosophy in which similar
           parts are identified and grouped together to take advantage of
           their similarities in design and production.

           Similar parts  part families

           Production equipment  machine groups or cells

    Each cell is specialized in production of a part family.

            Group technology is most appropriately applied under the
            following conditions:
                 the plant currently uses traditional batch production and
                  a process type layout;
                 the parts can be grouped into part families.
2/9/2026                                                                     5
            Tasks that a company must undertake to implement group
             technology(GT):
                identifying the part families,
                rearranging production machines into machine cells.


            The benefits of group technology:
                GT promotes standardization of tooling, fixturing, and setups
                material handling is reduced
                process planning and production scheduling are simplified
                setup times are reduced => lower manufacturing lead time
                work-in-process is reduced
                worker satisfaction usually improves
                higher quality work is accomplished

            Production flow analysis (PFA) is a method for identifying part
           families and associated machine groupings that uses the information
           contained on production route sheets rather than on part.
             PFA uses manufacturing data rather than design data, so two
              possible anomalies can occur in parts classification and coding:
                 parts whose basic geometries are quite different may
                  nevertheless require similar or even identical process routings.
                 parts whose geometries are quite similar may nevertheless
                  require process routings that are quite different.
             Steps for PFA:
                 data collection (part number, operation sequence, lot size, time
                  standards and annual demand)
                 sorting of process routings (parts are arranged into groups)
                 PFA chart (or part-machine incidence matrix)
2/9/2026         cluster analysis (related groupings are identified and rearranged)

            After part-machine groupings have been identified the
             problem is to organize the machines into the most logical
             arrangement.
            Hollier introduces six heuristic approaches to solving the
             machine arrangement problem.
            First two methods will be described using data contained in
             From-To charts.




2/9/2026                                                                   22
           The method requires next steps:
               1. Develop the From-To chart from part routing data;
               2. Determine the “From” and “To” sums for each machine;
               3. Assign machines to the cell based on minimum “From” or “To” sums:
                   • if the minimum value is a “To” sum, then the machine is placed at the
                       beginning of the sequence;
                   • if the minimum value is a “From” sum then the machine is placed at the end
                       of the sequence;
               Tie breaker rules:
                     • if a tie occurs between minimum “To” sums or minimum “From” sums =>
                       the machine with the minimum “From/To” ratio is selected;
                     • if both “To” and “From” sums are equal for a selected machine, it is passed
                       over and the machine with the next lowest sum is selected;
                     • if a minimum “To” sum is equal to a minimum “From” sum, then both
                       machines are selected and placed at the beginning and end of the sequence,
                       respectively.
               4. Reformat the From-To chart :
                     • after each machine has been selected, restructure the From-To chart by
                       eliminating the row and column corresponding to the selected machine and
                       recalculate the “From” and “To” sums;
2/9/2026             • repeat steps 3 and 4 until all machines have been assigned.          23
