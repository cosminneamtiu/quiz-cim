           Process planning is the activity of developing a manufacturing
           plan used to convert the product design into a physical entity.

Developing a process plan involves:
            - interpretation of design drawings,
            - determining the processes and sequence,
            - equipment selection,
            - tools, dies, molds, fixtures, and gauges selection,
            - analysis methods.
            - work standards,
            - cutting tools and cutting conditions, etc.

 The benefits of CAPP are:
       ´éº process rationalization and standardization
       ´éº increased productivity of process planners
       ´éº reduced lead time for process planning
       ´éº improved legibility
       ´éº incorporation of other application program

             Design for manufacturing and assembly (DFMA) is any procedure
             or design process that considers the production factors from the
             beginning of the product design.

´ü▒ DFMA provides the following typical advantages:

     ´éº Reduction of design-to-product launch cycles by as much as 50 percent.
     ´éº Reduction of part counts by 30 to 70 percent.
     ´éº Reduction of assembly times by 50 to 80 percent.


              ´ü▒ Design for manufacturing and assembly involves:
                     ´éº organizational changes
  2/9/2026
                     ´éº design principles and guidelines.                  19
           ´ü▒ Effective implementation of DFMA involves making changes in a
             companyÔÇÖs organizational structure:

                ´éº by creating project teams consisting of:
                    ´â╝ product designers
                    ´â╝ manufacturing engineers
                    ´â╝ quality engineers
                    ´â╝ material scientists

                ´éº by requiring design engineers to spend some time in
                  manufacturing;

                ´éº by assigning manufacturing engineers to the product design
                  department to serve as manufacturing consultants.

2/9/2026                                                                  20
       ´ü▒ General principles and guidelines in DFMA are:
           ´éº minimize number of components;
           ´éº use standard commercially available components;
           ´éº use common parts across product lines;
           ´éº design for ease of part fabrication;
           ´éº design parts with tolerances that are within process capability;
           ´éº design the product to be foolproof during assembly;
           ´éº minimize flexible components;
           ´éº design for ease of assembly;
           ´éº use modular design;
           ´éº shape parts and products for ease of packaging;
           ´éº eliminate or reduce adjustments.

             Design for quality (DFQ) is the term that refers to the principles
             and procedures employed to ensure that the highest possible
             quality is designed into the product.


´ü▒ The general objectives of DFQ are:
    ´éº to design the product to meet or exceed customer
      requirements

    ´éº to design the product so that its function and
      performance are relatively insensitive to variations in
      manufacturing and subsequent application.

    ´éº to continuously improve the performance, functionality,
      reliability, safety, and other quality aspects.

           Design for product cost (DFC) refers to the efforts of a company to

           manufactured and includes factors ranging from product delivery
