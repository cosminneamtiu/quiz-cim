     Order release provides the documentation needed to process a
     production order through the factory.

      Order release consists of:
                 route sheet – documents the process plan for the item to
                 be produced,
                 material requisitions – to draw the necessary raw
                 materials from inventory,
                 job cards – to report direct labor time devoted to the
                 order and to indicate progress of the order through the
                 factory,
                 move tickets – to authorize the material handling
                 personnel to transport parts between work centers,
                 parts list – if required for assembly jobs.

      Order scheduling assigns the production orders to the various
      work centers in the plant.

             prepares a dispatch list which indicates which production order

             provides information about relative priorities of the different jobs .

                     queue ratio rule

           The order progress module monitors the status of the various
           orders in the plant, work-in-process, and other characteristics
           that indicate the progress and performance of production.

            The information presented to production management is often
             summarized in the form of reports like:
                      work order status reports
                      progress reports
                      exception reports

            These reports are useful to production management in:
                      making decisions about allocation of resources;
                      authorization of overtime hours;
                      identifying problem areas in the plant that adversely affect
2/9/2026               achieving the MPS.                                    16
           The factory data collection system consists of the various paper
           documents, terminals, and automated devices located throughout the
           plant for collecting data on shop floor operations, plus the means for
           compiling and processing the data.



            The purpose of the factory data collection system is:
                     to supply status and performance data to the shop floor
                      control system;

                     to provide current information to production foremen,
                      plant management, and production control personnel.

            The types of inventory of greatest interest in PPC are:
                  raw materials,
                  purchased components,
                  in-process inventory (WIP),
                  finished products.
            The major costs of holding inventory are:
                  investment costs,
                  storage costs,
                  cost of possible obsolescence or spoilage

              Inventory control procedures:
                   for dependent demand – MRP is the most widely implemented
                    technique;
                   for independent demand – order point inventory systems are
                    used.

 Order points systems are concerned with two related problems that must be
  solved when managing inventories of independent demand items:
      how many units should be ordered? -> economic order quantity formulas,
      when should the order be placed? -> record point methods

           Manufacturing resource planning can be defined as a computer
           based system for planning, scheduling, and controlling the materials,
           resources, and supporting activities needed to meet the MPS.

 Application modules provided in a high-end MRP II system:
      management planning (business strategy, aggregate production
                                planning, MPS, budget planning)
      customer service (sales forecasting, order entry, sales analysis, finished
                          goods inventory)
      operations planning (MRP+ capacity requirements planning)
         operations execution (purchasing, production scheduling and control,
                                WIP inventory control, shop floor control, labor
                                hour tracking)
     financial functions ( cost accounting, accounts receivable, accounts
 2/9/2026                    payable)                                        20
        Benefits from MRP II:
             Reduction in inventory => less money spent on investments for
              material and the cost of keeping that material on the shelf until
              it is needed.
             Better planning in communications => reductions in the
              amount of inventory declared obsolete and written off as a loss.
             Better scheduling => allows better planning with suppliers and
              lower purchasing costs.
             Customer service levels are improved by shipping more
              complete orders on time and with improved responsiveness to
              customer requirements.

            Just-in-time production systems (JIT) were developed in Japan to
            minimize inventories, especially WIP.


            The ideal just-in-time production system produces and
             delivers exactly the required number of each component to the
             downstream operation in the manufacturing sequence just at
             the time when that component is needed.
            JIT operate successfully if we have:
                     a pull system of production control
                     small batch sizes
                     reduced setup times
2/9/2026             stable and reliable production operations         22
        JIT is based on a pull system of production control, in which the
          order to make and deliver parts at each workstation in the
          production sequence comes from the downstream station that
          uses those parts.
      One way to implement a pull system is to use kanbans. (kanban means
       card in Japanese).
      The Kanban system of production
       control, developed by Toyota, is based
       on the use of two types of cards that
       authorize parts production, and parts
       delivery in the plant:
            production kanbans (b)
            transport kanbans (a)
